Biology

Core Life Science Field Details

Field Summary

Biology is the **natural science that studies life and living organisms**. It covers their physical structure, chemical processes, molecular interactions, physiological mechanisms, development, and evolution. As a vast field, biology explores the unity and diversity of life on Earth, operating across all scales from **molecular mechanisms** within a cell to the global **interdependence of ecosystems**.

Key Sub-Fields

Microbiology

The study of **microscopic organisms**, including bacteria, viruses, archaea, fungi, and protozoa. It is vital for public health, immunology, biotechnology, and understanding **disease pathogenesis** (how diseases develop).

Genetics

The science of **heredity and the variation of organisms**. It focuses on genes, genetic variation, and heredity in living organisms. Modern genetics, including molecular genetics, drives biotechnology and personalized medicine.

Ecology

The study of **how organisms interact with each other and their surrounding environment**. It investigates the distribution and abundance of organisms and how ecosystems function, which is critical for conservation and understanding climate change impacts.

Cell Biology

Also known as **Cytology**. This field is dedicated to studying the **structure and function of the cell**, which is the fundamental unit of life. It encompasses cell cycles, division, signaling, and the movement of substances across the cell membrane.

Molecular Biology

Focuses on the **molecular basis of biological activity** within and between cells, particularly the interactions between DNA, RNA, and protein biosynthesis. This discipline links genetics with biochemistry to understand the complex machinery of life.
