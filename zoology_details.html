Zoology

Animal Science Field Details

Field Summary

Zoology is the biological discipline dedicated to the **study of the animal kingdom**. It is a broad field encompassing the **classification, anatomy, physiology, behavior, evolution, and ecology of all animals**, both vertebrate and invertebrate. Zoologists work to understand the structure of animal life, the mechanisms that govern their survival and reproduction, and the role animals play in the natural world.

Key Sub-Fields

Animal Physiology

Studies the **mechanical, physical, and biochemical functions** of animals, including organs, tissues, and cells. This includes understanding processes like respiration, circulation, nervous system control, and how animals adapt to changing environments.

Animal Anatomy

The study of the **internal and external structure** of animals. It includes gross anatomy (visible structures) and microscopic anatomy (histology and cytology), providing the foundational structural knowledge for all other animal sciences.

Ethology

The scientific and objective study of **animal behavior** in natural conditions. It examines behaviors such as communication, migration, mating rituals, and social structure, often linking them to evolutionary advantage.

Animal Ecology

Focuses on the **interactions of animals with their biotic and abiotic environments**. It analyzes population dynamics, community structure, species distribution, and the flow of energy through food webs.

Entomology

The specialized study of **insects**, which constitute the largest class in the animal kingdom. It is crucial for understanding pollination, pest control, disease transmission, and biodiversity conservation.

Ichthyology

The scientific study of **fish**, including bony fish, cartilaginous fish, and jawless fish. It covers their anatomy, behavior, ecology, and role in aquatic ecosystems and fisheries management.

Herpetology

Dedicated to the study of **amphibians** (frogs, salamanders) and **reptiles** (snakes, turtles, lizards). This field is critical for conservation efforts due to the high vulnerability of these groups to environmental change.

Mammalogy

The branch of zoology that focuses on the **class Mammalia**. It studies the anatomy, physiology, behavior, ecology, and evolution of mammals, including primates, whales, and rodents.
