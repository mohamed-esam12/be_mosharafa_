Geology

Earth Science Field Details

Field Summary

Geology is the **study of the Earth**, its structure, materials (minerals and rocks), and the processes that have shaped it over geological time. Geologists investigate phenomena from plate tectonics and volcanic activity to the formation of mountains and the distribution of natural resources. Geology provides critical insights into the Earth's past history, including climate change, and guides the sustainable extraction of water and energy resources.

Key Sub-Fields

Mineralogy

The scientific study of **minerals**, including their crystal structure, chemical composition, and physical properties. This knowledge is essential because minerals are the basic building blocks of rocks and are the sources of metals and many industrial materials.

Petrology

The study of **rocks**—igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary—including their origin, composition, texture, and structure. Petrologists use this information to understand the processes occurring within the Earth's crust and mantle.

Structural Geology

Investigates the **three-dimensional geometry and deformation** of rock masses. It studies folds, faults, and other features resulting from tectonic forces, which is key to finding oil, gas, and understanding earthquake hazards.

Stratigraphy

Deals with the **succession and age of rock layers (strata)**. By analyzing the vertical layering of rocks, stratigraphers establish geological timelines and correlate rock units across vast distances, crucial for petroleum exploration.

Sedimentology

The study of **sediments and the processes of deposition** that lead to the formation of sedimentary rocks. It examines how erosion, transport, and settling create formations that often host groundwater, oil, and gas.

Paleontology

The scientific study of **ancient life** based on **fossil evidence**. Paleontologists use fossils to reconstruct evolutionary history, understand ancient environments, and determine the age of the rocks in which the fossils are found.

Economic Geology

Focuses on Earth materials that can be used for **economic or industrial purposes**, such as metal ores, non-metallic minerals, building materials, petroleum, coal, and geothermal energy sources. This field drives exploration and mining efforts.

Engineering Geology

The application of geological principles to **engineering practice**. It investigates the geological factors affecting the planning, design, construction, operation, and maintenance of structures like dams, bridges, tunnels, and skyscrapers.
