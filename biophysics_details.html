Biophysics

Interdisciplinary Science Field Details

Field Summary

Biophysics is the interdisciplinary science that **applies theories and methods of physics to study biological systems**. It seeks to understand how biological molecules work, how cells function mechanistically, and how energy is transduced in living organisms. By viewing life as a physical system, biophysics provides quantitative explanations for biological processes, from the folding of proteins to the propagation of nerve impulses.

Key Sub-Fields

Molecular Biophysics

Focuses on the **structure, dynamics, and interactions of biological macromolecules** (like DNA, RNA, and proteins). It uses techniques such as X-ray crystallography and cryo-electron microscopy to understand how a protein's physical shape dictates its function within the cell.

Cellular Biophysics

Examines the **mechanical and electrical properties of cells and tissues**. This includes studying the **fluidity of cell membranes**, the physical forces involved in cell division and migration, and the electrical signals responsible for nerve and muscle function.

Medical & Radiation Biophysics

Applies physical principles to **medical technology, diagnosis, and treatment**. This involves the physics behind medical imaging (MRI, PET scans), the study of how **radiation affects biological tissue** (radiotherapy), and the development of new biosensors and diagnostic devices.
