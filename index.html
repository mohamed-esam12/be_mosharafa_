Explore the Core Fields of Science

A summary of the 12 foundational and modern scientific disciplines.

Physical Sciences üåå

Cosmic Study

Astronomy

The study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole, including its origin and evolution.

Core Science

Physics

The natural science that studies matter, its motion and behavior through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force.

Core Science

Chemistry

The science of the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter, including its atoms and molecules.

Interdisciplinary

Biophysics

The interdisciplinary science that applies the theories and methods of physics to study biological systems and problems.

Life Sciences üß¨

Core Life Science

Biology

The study of life and living organisms, including their physical structure, chemical processes, molecular interactions, physiological mechanisms, development, and evolution.

Animal Study

Zoology

The biological study of the animal kingdom, including the structure, embryology, evolution, classification, habits, and distribution of all animals, both living and extinct.

Plant Study

Botany

The scientific study of plants, including their structure, properties, and biochemical processes, as well as the classification of and relationships among plant groups.

Applied Science

Biotechnology

The use of living systems and organisms to develop or make products, or any technological application that uses biological systems.

Formal Science

Mathematics

The study of quantity, structure, space, and change. It is the language of science and a fundamental discipline for analysis.

Formal Science

Computer Science

The study of computation, automation, and information, including theoretical foundations and algorithmic thinking.

Earth Science

Geology

The study of the Earth, the materials of which it is made, the structure of those materials, and the processes acting upon them.

Earth Science

Geophysics

The study of the physical Earth and its environment, using principles of physics, including the Earth's interior, magnetic field, and seismology.
